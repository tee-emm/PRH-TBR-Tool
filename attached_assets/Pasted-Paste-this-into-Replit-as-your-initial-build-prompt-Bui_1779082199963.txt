- Make everything responsive, accessible, keyboard-friendly, and fast.
- Include reduced-motion support.
- Use warm, bookish, visually polished styling.
- Use a small hardcoded dataset of sample PRH catalogue titles.
- Each book object should include: title, author, category, mood tags, discovery channels, TBR triggers, cover image URL, PRH catalogue URL, reader hook, discovery path, and positioning angle.
- Use manually pasted PRH catalogue cover URLs as placeholders for now.

VISUAL DIRECTION:
The site should be very eye-catching but buildable. Use:
- Warm paper background
- Bold editorial serif headline
- Clean sans-serif UI text
- Colourful book cards
- Soft shadows
- Rounded corners
- CSS sticker labels
- Catalogue tags
- Staff-pick badges
- Animated “MATCHED” stamp
- Sliding card reveal
- Receipt printer effect
- A polished, slightly cute bookstore/catalogue feeling

Do not make it look childish. It should feel charming, literary, thoughtful, and application-appropriate.

SITE STRUCTURE:

1. HERO SECTION
Title:
“Build Your PRH Front Table”

Subtitle:
“A tiny catalogue-powered reader discovery tool by Thomass Muir.”

Intro copy:
“I learn about books through patterns: the review that gives context, the reader reaction that gives urgency, the bookstore table that creates serendipity, and the interview that makes a book feel alive. This matchmaker turns those discovery habits into a small PRH starter shelf.”

Button:
“Start Matching”

Ask the user three questions.

Question 1:
“What reading mood are you in?”
Options:
- Haunting
- Expansive
- Tender
- Strange
- Smart
- Cozy
- Hungry
- Book-club ready

Question 2:
“Where would this book most likely find you?”
Options:
- Bookstore table
- Long-form review
- Friend recommendation
- Author interview
- Newsletter
- Reader community
- Social media rabbit hole

Question 3:
“What makes you add a book to your TBR?”
Options:
- Voice
- Premise
- Cover
- Cultural conversation
- Emotional reaction
- Comparison titles
- Author story

As the user answers, show a small progress shelf or selection summary:
Mood: [selected]
Channel: [selected]
Trigger: [selected]

3. MATCHED RESULT
After the user answers all three questions, animate a “MATCHED” stamp and reveal:

“Your PRH Starter Shelf”

Show 3 matched book cards from the hardcoded dataset. Matching should be based on overlap between the user’s selected mood, channel, and trigger.

Each book card should include:
- Cover image
- Category badge
- Title
- Author
- Why this matched
- Discovery path
- How I’d position it
- “View in PRH Catalogue” link

Example fields:
Why this matched:
“This pick fits your interest in voice-driven, emotionally resonant books with strong reader conversation potential.”

Discovery path:
“I’d expect this to travel through book clubs, long-form reviews, and heartfelt friend recommendations.”

How I’d position it:
“Lead with emotional intimacy, memory, and the kind of story readers want to pass along.”

- Buttons must be real buttons.
- Cards and modals must be keyboard accessible.
- Images must have alt text.
- Colour contrast should be readable.

- Add focus states.
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion.
